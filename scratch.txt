# Institutional Trading Systems

I have a deep understanding of regulated, high-stakes systems: At Merrill Lynch I supported the *Order Audit Trail System* (OATS); a compliance system that NASDAQ requires large institutions to adhere to that is supposed to ensure that the firms aren't cheating e.g. by noting that a bunch of their clients are buying a particular stock, and then *getting in on the action*. There are strict rules about that. OATS is designed to expose the patterns that indicate that those sorts of activities are noticed.

When I worked for CIBC, I led a team that supported the bank's automated (no touch) FOREX trading system. My team was an elite team tasked with supporting mission critical systems where downtime would have a direct effect on the revenue stream of the bank. The runbook for that system stated that the system generated a cool 4 million CAD an hour, up to 40 million when there was a surge when markets opened.

That experience planted a seed. I spent some time reearching FOREX trading and automated trading systems. What I concluded at the time was that this venture was out of reach for consumers. Most automated FOREX trading systems rely on rules like, *when selling 500 units, break the trade into smaller blocks; statistically the return is higher*. The margins on those sort of trades are tiny. What makes it profitable is that the algorithms are fairly predictable, and -most importantly- that the bank is betting with a pool of millions of dollars. So *"tiny"* profits add up. A trader sitting at home, betting with her savings, doesn't have access to that sort of capital. The other *edge* that large institutions have is that they locate their trading systems adjacent to the core FOREX trading systems.

# The Lightbulb Turns On

Then, I came accross a very, very extensive YouTube video series, [Forex bot & backtest system with Python for beginners.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zKk2iuuNJO0&list=PLZ1QII7yudbecO6a-zAI6cuGP1LLnmW8e&index=1). It consists of 83 videos and covers everything from setting up an [OANDA](https://www.oanda.com/ca-en/]) account through to creating a bot that trades based on some basic trading strategies. 

A flash bulb went off and I thought, "Hey, why not replace or augment those trading strategies with a neural network." While I'm not very optimistic that I will be able to develop a profitable system, I am 100% certain that I will learn a LOT by going through the process. I also think there's a chance that it may work. My lean, fast iterative style combined with a strategy that only exectutes trades when there's a *recognised window of opportunity* may turn into a small passive income stream. The increadible wealth of historical FOREX trading data also screams, *curated training data* to me.

So, I setup a [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/NadimGhaznavi/aifx), and created a basic project framework that included a [GitHub Action](https://github.com/NadimGhaznavi/aifx/blob/main/.github/workflows/publish-to-pypi.yml) to publish to [PyPI](https://pypi.org/project/aifx/), and a bare bones skeleton project. 

The seed has been planted. Let's see what I can grow!
